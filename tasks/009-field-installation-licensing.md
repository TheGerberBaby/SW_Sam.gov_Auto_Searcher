## Why this matters

Camera, access-control, and cabling notices are only useful if Stormwind can
legally perform and warranty the work. Virginia uses separate contractor and
electronic-security rules, so this must be confirmed before pricing Virginia
installations.

## Official starting points

- Virginia DPOR contractor licensing step guide:
  [Contractors Step Guide](https://www.dpor.virginia.gov/sites/default/files/boards/Contractors/Contractors_Step_Guide.pdf)
- Virginia DPOR specialty definitions:
  [Contractor Specialty Definitions](https://www.dpor.virginia.gov/Boards/Contractors/Definitions)
- Virginia DCJS electronic-security overview:
  [Electronic Security Services](https://www.dcjs.virginia.gov/licensure-and-regulatory-affairs/electronic-security)
- Virginia DCJS business requirements:
  [Electronic Security Business](https://www.dcjs.virginia.gov/licensure-and-regulatory-affairs/electronic-security/electronic-security-business)
- Virginia DCJS technician requirements:
  [Electronic Security Personnel](https://www.dcjs.virginia.gov/licensure-and-regulatory-affairs/electronic-security/electronic-security-personnel)

## Working assumptions to verify with the agencies

- Virginia DPOR's `ESC` specialty covers electronic and fiber-optic
  communication-system work such as data communications.
- Camera, intrusion, and access-control work may trigger Virginia DCJS
  electronic-security business and technician-registration requirements.
- Jobs with electrical-power or fire-alarm integration may require additional
  classifications, permits, or licensed partners.
- Out-of-state jobs require a jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction check.

These are planning assumptions, not claimed licenses or legal advice.

## Subtasks

- [ ] Ask DPOR which contractor class and specialties apply to Cat6, fiber,
  CCTV, access-control, and any low-voltage work Stormwind plans to self-perform
- [ ] Ask DCJS which electronic-security business license and individual
  registrations apply to camera, intrusion, and access-control installation
- [ ] Decide whether power connections and fire-alarm integration will be
  excluded, self-performed under an applicable license, or subcontracted
- [ ] Obtain quotes for general liability, workers' compensation if applicable,
  vehicle/tools coverage, and installation warranty exposure
- [ ] Build a one-page jurisdiction checklist for Virginia, Maryland, DC, and
  out-of-area federal jobs
- [ ] Record confirmed licenses, registrations, insurance limits, renewal
  dates, and approved scope in [PROFILE.md](../PROFILE.md)

## Acceptance criteria

Stormwind has a written jurisdiction checklist and a documented decision for
the licenses, technician registrations, insurance, and subcontractor
boundaries required before bidding field installations.
